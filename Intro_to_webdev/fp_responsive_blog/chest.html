How To Grow
Upper And Lower Chest

December 8th, 2021

- Jack McMillan

The goal of this article is to help you understand how to best work the parts of your chest to increase mass and volume. The chest is separated into 3 different parts; upper, middle, and lower. A majority of the time the exercises you do focus on 2 of the 3 groups so you don't need to work the muscle groups individually. Depending on your experience with resistance training when starting with chest exercises you should try the lowest weight and run through the motions to better understand what your limits are.

Upper Chest

The upper chest, or clavicular head, can be focused in a series of exercises. Regardless of whether you have been in the gym for a long time, or just starting, everyone can have difficulties building the upper chest if not doing it right. If you were to ask any bodybuilder the key to building an upper chest they would say, incline bench and chest flys. That is correct but to some extent, these exercises hyper-focus on the muscle fibers which come with high risk because if done incorrectly can cause extreme injuries. The best, safe, exercises consist of low-to-high cable raises, converging incline machine press, and dumbbell incline press.

Lower Chest

The lower chest can be ignored in a lot of people's workouts because they are too focused on the shape. Although this is true the lower chest or costal head makes up a majority of the muscle in the pectoralis major. Almost any motion that lifts something in front of your body uses a portion of the lower chest. Therefore it should never be skipped in a workout due to it being used so much. If you are at home you can do exercises like incline pushups or parallel dips on pieces of furniture. If you have access to a gym some other alternatives would be decline barbell bench press or cable crossovers.

In conclusion, the chest is a series of very complicated muscles that are very commonly misinterpreted. If you're going strictly for shape and size, the upper and middle chest is where you want to be training. The negative of this would be that you would be losing out on a lot of mass and strength in your lower chest. If it were up to me I would put everyone on an even workout with safe exercises. This would help people build both mass, volume, and build muscle in moderation. So you can always look amazing and feel confident too!
