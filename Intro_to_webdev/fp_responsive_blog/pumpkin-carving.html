How To
Carve A Pumpkin

October 27nd, 2021

Halloween is a very special time of year where people of all ages can come together to do activities that usually can only be done in the fall season. This article will highlight how to carve a pumpkin with any design you like. This will keep your Halloween spirit by ensuring you can participate in this Halloween activity with family and friends.

Find a template of design you would like to carve

The easiest way of making sure you have the best possible outcome for your pumpkin is by following a design/template. There are plenty of templates online that are easily printable and can be either traced or taped onto the side of the pumpkin to make it easy to carve without making a mistake.

Purchase pumpkins that are an appropriate size for design

Pumpkin size is very important, so try to find one that fits the dimensions of your design and that can stand up on its own. (Some people recommend purchasing one that has a flat side making it easy to carve and write on.)

Prepping pumpkin

Prepping the pumpkin is an essential step in making the pumpkin carving process as easy as possible. You start with carving a circle around the stem of the pumpkin at a 45° angle to ensure that the stem does not fall inside of the pumpkin when you cut out the stem. Next, you want to take out all of the seeds and “guts” inside of the pumpkin. Lastly, it is recommended to carve a small layer off the sides of the walls of the pumpkin so that it is cleaner, and can glow easier in the dark when a candle is placed inside.

Drawing or taping the design on

This step is not mandatory but is recommended a majority of the time. By either drawing or taping your design onto the side of the pumpkin you are making the carving process a lot easier to follow and reduces the chances of mistakes.

Carving out your design

Find a sharp knife and use a gentle back and forth motion along the line of your design until you reach a corner of the design. When cutting around corners you should remove your knife from the pumpkin and stab back into it in the intended direction to avoid round edges/corners. If a piece of the pumpkin does not fall out immediately when finishing cutting, you can stick your hand in the top of the pumpkin and push it out from the inside.

Lighting up your pumpkin

Lastly, find a candle or plastic fake candle and place it inside of your pumpkin. Put the top on top of the pumpkin, turn off the lights, and you have your finished, carved, pumpkin!

Lastly, a little bonus tip that can be used to have a fun snack is seasoning and cooking the pumpkin seeds in the oven. It calls for a good thing to nibble on throughout the night. Now that you are a master of pumpkin carving, you can always be prepared to impress your friends and family with your new skills!
